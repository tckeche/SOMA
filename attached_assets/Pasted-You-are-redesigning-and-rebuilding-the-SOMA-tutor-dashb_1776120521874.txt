You are redesigning and rebuilding the SOMA tutor dashboard and the tutor facing student profile experience.

Act as a senior product designer, senior UX strategist, senior frontend engineer, data visualization designer, and AI assisted analytics engineer.

This is not a cosmetic redesign.
This is a full rethink of the tutor operating experience.

I want SOMA to feel like a premium academic control centre for a serious tutor.
It must feel elegant, robust, sophisticated, glassy, data rich, and deeply purposeful.
It must not feel gimmicky, empty, decorative, or like a student portal template.

You must rebuild the tutor dashboard and the student profile page around one principle:

Every component must help the tutor teach better, assess better, intervene faster, or understand the student more clearly.

If a section does not help the tutor do that, remove it.

Core product philosophy

The tutor dashboard is a command centre.
It is not a decorative analytics page.
It is not a feed.
It is not a vague overview.
It must immediately tell the tutor:

1. What needs action now
2. Which students need intervention and why
3. Which submissions need marking
4. Which assessments are outstanding
5. How the cohort is performing
6. Where topic weakness is recurring
7. What the tutor should do next

The student profile page is a diagnostic teaching workspace.
It must show real academic evidence, not gimmicks.
It must help the tutor understand:
1. How the student is performing
2. Which topics are weak
3. Which topics are improving
4. What has been covered
5. What has not been covered
6. What kind of intervention is needed next

Non negotiable design direction

The visual design must be:
1. Bold
2. Elegant
3. Striking
4. Robust
5. Sophisticated
6. Premium
7. Glassmorphic, but readable and controlled

I want stronger colours, richer depth, better composition, more intentional glow, more refined gradients, better contrast, stronger card separation, and more luxurious spacing.

Do not make it noisy.
Do not make it cyberpunk.
Do not make it childish.
Do not make it gimmicky.

Think premium dark mode academic intelligence platform with confident glass surfaces and precise information hierarchy.

Glassmorphism requirements

Use layered glass panels with:
1. Soft but controlled blur
2. Deeper translucent navy and midnight bases
3. Carefully chosen accent colours for meaning
4. Inner highlights and refined borders
5. Stronger shadow separation between layers
6. High readability at all times

Maintain proper contrast for text, labels, chips, charts, icons, and controls.
Do not let glow or blur reduce legibility.
The interface must feel premium and serious, not hazy or washed out.

Information architecture to implement

PART 1. Tutor dashboard redesign

Rebuild the tutor dashboard into these purposeful sections.

Section A. Strategic top band

At the very top, create a powerful summary band that surfaces the tutor’s critical operating state immediately.

This must include:
1. Active students
2. Assessments awaiting submission
3. Reviews pending
4. Cohort average
5. Completion rate this week
6. Students below threshold
7. Fastest falling topic or weakest cohort topic

These should not be generic stat cards.
Design them as premium command tiles with:
1. Strong hierarchy
2. Bold numbers
3. Short labels
4. Tiny sparkline or trend cue where appropriate
5. Beautiful hover and reveal motion
6. Immediate readability

The tutor must understand the state of the cohort in under five seconds.

Section B. Intervention Queue

Do not call this “students needing attention.”

Label it something stronger and more purposeful such as:
Intervention Queue
Priority Learners
Teaching Focus
Intervention Board

Choose the best title, but keep it premium and serious.

This is the hero section of the dashboard.

Only surface students who matter right now.
Do not show everyone.
Rank and display the top few students requiring intervention based on real data.

The ranking model must use real platform data such as:
1. Falling scores
2. Repeated weakness in the same topic
3. Growing awaiting workload
4. Low completion rate
5. Missing submissions
6. Deteriorating trend
7. Low confidence in recently tested topics

For each surfaced student show:
1. Full name
2. Risk or priority status
3. Short AI assisted explanation of why the student appears here, based only on real data
4. Recurring weak topics
5. Assigned, completed, awaiting counts
6. Submission reliability
7. Trend direction
8. Last activity
9. Quick actions, View Profile, Assign Work, Review Latest, Message if applicable

This section must feel like a real intervention surface, not a list of bars.

Design approach:
1. Use a refined stacked card layout or split hero panel
2. Each student card should feel substantial, elegant, and information rich
3. Use mini visualizations intelligently
4. Do not use those current long empty bars that say very little
5. Use stronger color meaning, red, amber, green, indigo, but in a premium restrained way

Section C. Review Queue and Submission Flow

Create a highly usable review area that helps the tutor move directly into marking.

This must show:
1. Newest submissions
2. Pending review items
3. Assessment title
4. Student
5. Subject and level
6. Submission time
7. Time spent if available
8. Score if already auto marked
9. Review priority
10. Quick action to open

This should be one of the most actionable sections on the page.

Use a two state design such as:
Review Queue
Recent Submissions

Make it elegant, scannable, and operational.

Section D. Cohort Signals

Create a clean but powerful cohort insight area.

Only include visuals that genuinely help teaching decisions.

This section should contain:
1. Subject performance overview
2. Weak topic leaderboard across the cohort
3. Cohort trend over recent assessments
4. Completion trend or participation signal

Do not overload this area.
Make it clean and sharp.

Preferred chart forms:
1. Refined vertical bar chart for subject averages
2. Elegant line or area chart for performance trend
3. Ranked topic weakness panel
4. Compact contribution or variance visuals where helpful

Every chart must have a clear question it answers.

Section E. Cohort Workload Matrix

The tutor needs to see workload clearly, but the current table is too flat and lifeless.

Redesign this as a more premium and useful operational matrix.

For each student show:
1. Name
2. Assigned
3. Completed
4. Awaiting
5. Completion rate
6. Last activity
7. Current trend
8. Quick access to profile

This must be visually stronger and easier to scan.

Do not show raw text only.
Use elegant embedded visuals such as:
1. Mini segmented workload bars
2. Completion ring plus count
3. Tiny trend sparkline
4. Status chip
5. Compact heat indicators

Design it as a serious operations table with premium styling, not a plain admin grid.

Only show names, no email addresses.

Section F. Quick actions

Keep a compact action area with:
1. Create Assessment
2. Assign to Students
3. Review Submissions
4. View Students
5. Add Student

These actions should feel premium, weighted, and obvious.

Section G. Recent assessments

Redesign this section completely.

Show recent assessments as compact premium cards or a refined horizontal list.

For each assessment show:
1. Title
2. Subject
3. Level
4. Number assigned

PART 2. Student profile redesign

The student profile page must be rebuilt as a proper academic profile, not a gimmicky page.

It must feel like a diagnostic workspace for the tutor.

Structure the profile clearly into these areas.

Section A. Student identity header

Show:
1. Full name
2. Subject and level context where relevant
3. Overall current standing
4. Recent trend
5. Completion reliability
6. Average score
7. Last activity
8. Priority flag if needed

This header must feel premium and clear.

Section B. Performance by topic

The current bar graph that says little must go.

Replace it with a serious and interpretable topic performance view.

This section must show:
1. Topic name
2. Average score on that topic
3. Number of assessments touching that topic
4. Trend for that topic
5. Confidence or evidence strength
6. Whether the topic is improving, stable, or declining

Preferred visual form:
A ranked horizontal performance table with embedded bars, trend arrows, evidence counts, and score bands.

This is better than a decorative chart because it actually says something.

The tutor must instantly see:
1. Strongest topics
2. Weakest topics
3. Most volatile topics
4. Topics with too little evidence

Section C. Coverage intelligence

Coverage graphs must be properly made.

Do not create fake or decorative coverage visuals.

Coverage must be computed from real syllabus mapping and real assessment data, including:
1. Which topics have been assessed
2. How often they have been assessed
3. How recently they have been assessed
4. The depth of assessment coverage
5. The student’s performance within those topics

I want this area to feel premium and analytically serious.

Use a dual system:

1. A beautiful syllabus coverage overview visual
2. A detailed topic coverage matrix below it

For the overview visual:
1. Use a polygon or radar style chart only if the syllabus topic count is low enough for clarity
2. If the topic count is too high, automatically switch to a more readable segmented radial or matrix view
3. Never force a radar chart where it becomes unreadable
4. The chart must look high quality, deliberate, and refined
5. Use layered glass and premium glow treatments carefully

For the detailed coverage matrix:
Show each major topic with:
1. Coverage percentage
2. Number of questions attempted
3. Number of assessments touching the topic
4. Last assessed date
5. Student performance in that topic
6. Confidence of the coverage estimate

This section must be one of the strongest parts of the profile.

Section D. Assessment history

Show a clean evidence based timeline of recent assessments with:
1. Title
2. Date
3. Score
4. Time spent
5. Topics covered
6. Tutor feedback snippet
7. Quick open action

Section E. AI academic summary

Use AI to help interpret the real data, but not to invent data.

This must be a serious assistive layer, not a guess generator.

The AI model may use platform data to generate:
1. Concise performance narrative
2. Recurrent weakness summary
3. Improvement trend summary
4. Suggested teaching focus areas
5. Suggested next assessment target
6. Suggested intervention priority

Critical rule:
AI must only interpret actual platform data.
It must not fabricate trends, scores, weaknesses, or recommendations.
Every AI statement must be traceable to real inputs in the system.

This AI layer must be human in the loop.
The tutor remains the final decision maker.
The AI should support interpretation, not replace judgment.

PART 3. AI assisted dashboard intelligence

I want AI in the dashboard, but used properly.

Use ChatGPT or another suitable model as an intelligence and explanation layer over real website data.

AI should help with:
1. Ranking intervention priority
2. Summarizing risk factors
3. Explaining why a learner is in the intervention queue
4. Summarizing topic weakness recurrence
5. Generating short narrative insights from actual performance data
6. Recommending where tutor attention should go next

AI must not:
1. Guess missing facts
2. Invent trends
3. Hallucinate weak topics
4. Replace actual analytics logic
5. Drive decisions without transparent evidence

Build the AI system so that:
1. Raw metrics are computed deterministically from the platform database
2. AI receives those real metrics as input
3. AI produces explanation and prioritization support
4. The tutor can always inspect the underlying signals
5. The output is overridable and transparent
